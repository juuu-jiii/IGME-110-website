INSTAGRAM

Instagram feeds euphemise advertisements under the misleading term "sponsored post". Sponsored posts appear, on average, after every five "regular" non-sponsored posts on the feed, [1] and can be for nearly anything fathomable.

A "sponsored post" for a skateboard. I do not know how to skateboard, nor have I ever Googled anything related to one in the past, ever.
A "sponsored post" for Del Monte foods. I am not familiar with this brand
A "sponsored post" for glass products. I am clumsy and have not purchased any glass-related products.
A "sponsored post" for a novel, presumably about hatred and other vile behaviour associated with such an emotion. It has been years since I read a novel.

Besides sponsored posts, Instagram features advertisements on their Stories page as well. They are also labelled ambiguously as "sponsored". It has been reported that sponsored posts appear after viewing approximately three people's Stories [2].

A "sponsored" Story for Arby's. I do not like eating fast food.
A "sponsored" Story for diapers. I prefer regular underwear.
A "sponsored" Story for a shoe brand. I am not currently looking for a pair of shoes.

Two things are evident about the above images:

  1. They are screenshots from my own Instagram account.
  2. They do not suit my personal preferences.

In fact, this correlates with a common complaint among Instagrammers. Many users express discontent regarding the impersonality of its advertising system. [3]
