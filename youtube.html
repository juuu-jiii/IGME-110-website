YOUTUBE

YouTube advertisements have been present for much longer than most other forms of advertising on social media. YouTube's recent addition of mid-roll advertisements means that videos over 10 minutes long can feature advertisements in the middle of these videos [4]. These advertisements can take on multiple forms:

Two of the types of mid-roll advertisements:
  1. video advertisements - interrupt video when playing; sometimes skippable
  2. display advertisements - appear on right side
Two of the types of mid-roll advertisements:
  1. overlay advertisements (translucent) - appear on lower portion of video
  2. display advertisements - appear on the right
Two of the types of mid-roll advertisements:
  1. overlay advertisements (opaque) - appear on lower portion of video
  2. display advertisements - appear on right side

Notice how the display advertisements on the right keep changing. They reflect all advertisement breaks sprinkled throughout the video.

There exists a relationship between the length of a video and the number of mid-roll advertisements it may contain. Generally, the longer a video is, the more advertisements it can hold; YouTube allows one mid-roll break after approximately one minute of video [5]. The mid-roll advertisements are represented by small yellow bars in the scrubber.

YouTuber Danielle Cohn's video from May 2018, containing 1 mid-roll advertisement.
YouTuber Danielle Cohn's video from October 2019, containing 6 mid-roll advertisements.

It thus follows that this system is susceptible to abuse. Despite there existing a limit on the number of advertisements per minute video, the video's length itself is unlimited, meaning theoretically that the number of mid-roll advertisements is also, effectively, infinite overall.

Some well-known repercussions of the mid-roll advertisement system:

  • they can be jarring [6]
  • they are often awkward [7]
  • they can be interruptive [8]
